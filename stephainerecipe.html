Grandma's Turkey Dressing

by Stephaine Gordon

Ingredients

  • Turkey broth
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 box of cornbread (cooked)
  • 1 can of biscuits (cooked)
  • 1 onion, cut into pieces
  • Poultry seasoning (to taste, or until your ancestors say to stop
  • Turkey

Directions

The first time I learned how to make this from my grandmother, she gave me the vaguest directions. This is the best interpretation of that I have been able to write down.

  1. Cook your cornbread and biscuits separately, according to package directions.
  2. Either boil a turkey thigh or use packaged turkey broth. Boil your eggs with in the turkey broth.
  3. Let everything cool enough to handle before crumbling the cornbread, biscuits, conion, eggs, and turkey in a deep casserole pan.
  4. Add turkey broth until everything is barely submerged.
  5. Add poultry seasoning to taste and mix it in.
  6. Cook on 400°F until it is hot and is not watery. You'll want it to jiggle like jelly and have a brown crust.

Down South Louisiana Crawfish Boil

by Kim Hobley

Ingredients

  • The Star of the Show:
    • 30-35 lb. sack of live crawfish
  • "You betta season that water!"
    • 6 oranges (cut into halves)
    • 6 lemons (cut into halves)
    • 1 stalk of celery
    • 6 bell peppers (quartered)
    • 12 peeled garlic cloves
    • 3 yellow onionss (quartered)
    • 3 purple onions (quartered)
    • One whole pineapple (chunked)
    • 1/2 gallon of pineapple juice
    • 4.5 lbs bag of Louisiana Crawfish, Shrimp, and Crab Boil Seasoning
    • Fresh Herbs: Thyme, Bay Leaves, Basil, and Oregano
  • Soakin' dem dere Mudbugs
    • 24 ears of frozen corn (mini or tot size)
    • 40 oz Budlight
    • 1 stick of unsalted butter
  • Lagniappe Ingredients:
    • shrimp
    • potatoes
    • turkey necks
    • sausage

Directions

I cook my boil in an outdoor 60qt Boiling Pot, with a cooking basket, so that I can cook the entire sack all at once, but use what you have readily available. The core ingredients and seasoning canbe used for at least one more sack of the crawfish.

  1. Fill your pot until only 2-3 hole lines of holes on your basket are showing. If you need a more specific measurement, the pot should have a maximum fill line inside the pot.
  2. Rest the basket on the basket rests so that it isn't fully submerged because now "you betta season that water!" Take that list of ingredients and put all of them into the basket and put the basket back into the pot.
  3. Fire up the pot and boil the seasonings for at least 30-45 minutes, or until they are falling apart.If you want to add in Lagniappe ingredients (all EXCEPT the shrimp), the time do so when you have about 15 more minutes left in this part of the boiling process.
      4. While your are boiling the seasonings you should be purging (cleaning your crawfish). I use a crawfih washer, but place them in a cooler or a large bucket and use the hose to clean them. kind of stir the water so that the dirt starts to come off of the crawfish. Continue this process until the water runs clear. Keep them in the water until it is time for them to cook or at least keep them in a shaded area. When it's time to pour them in the pot, make sure you have already poured out the water and any sediment from the cleaning. A pro tip is to clean them in a cooler that has a spout at the bottom for drainage. Place it at an angle so the water can flow out.
  4. Once those ingredients have cooked down, remove everything from the pot and dump them in your cooler or wherever you plan on putting the crawfish at once you are finished. Check to make sure any additions like the turkey necks and potatoes are done. If they aren't they should remain in the pot so they can continue to cook while the crawfish is cooking.
  5. Now, it is time for "The Star of the Show" For this part take out your timer because you DO NOT want to mess this step up. Your basket should be resting on the rests. Pour the entire sack of crawfish into your basket.
    6. Drop the basket into the boiling water, place the lid on the pot and set your timer for EXACTLY 7 minutes.
  6. When the timer goes off, turn off your heating source. Now it is time to "soak dem dere mudbugs." Add the Budlight, stick of butter, and the 24 ears of corn. Feel free to add some of the seasoings you took out of the pot earlier.If you are adding raw shrimp, this is the time to do so.
  7. Place the lid on the pot and let the crawfish soak for 45 minutes to an hour making sure that you stir the pot every 15 minutes. I usually soak mine for an hour.
  8. When they have soaked up all that good flavor, remove them from the pot and serve.

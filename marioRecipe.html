Perfect Smoked Brisket

by: Mario Cabrera

Ingredients

  • 1 whole brisket (9-12lbs)
  • Salt (Anything except for sea-salt)
  • Lillie's Q Brisket Pepper
  • Heinz Yellow Mustard
  • 1 Bag of large coals
  • 1 Bag of large cut hickory wood
  • 1 spray bottle
  • 1 cup of Motts Apple Juice
  • 1/4 cup of Apple Cider Vinegar
  • Butcher paper

Directions

Take the brisket and trim the fat until you are left with a little over 1/4 of an inch of fat on the fat cap. Be sure to trim off any parts of hard fat. Once the brisket is room temperature, cover the entire brisket with yellow mustard to use as a binder for the seasonings. Cover the entire brisket in a 50/50 ratio of salt and pepper. Be sure to cover every part of the brisket. Once seasoned, prepare the smoker by lighting coals and allowing the smoker to get to around 180 degrees. Once the smoker is the right temperature, add the pieces of hickory wood on top of the coals, and then place the brisket in the smoker. While the brisket cooks, fill up the spray bottle with an 8:2 ratio of apple juice to apple cider vinegar. Be sure to spray the brisket with this mixture every 45 minutes. Once the brisket reaches an internal temp of 200 degrees, remove it from the smoker and wrap it in the butcher paper. Let it rest for 2 hours. After it has rested, cut into it and enjoy!
